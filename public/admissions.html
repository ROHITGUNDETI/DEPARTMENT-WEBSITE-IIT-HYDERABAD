Asif Qureshi - A Global model for marine bioaccumulation of trace contaminants in a changing climate Marine species are an important source of proteins and nutrients worldwide. However, they also bioaccumulate certain trace contaminants. Hence benefits of nutrient intake are negated by the harms of consuming trace contaminants. While there are international treaties to reduce emissions, there are other factors such as climate change and other anthropogenic impacts that affect both species’ physiology and the food-web. Thus, the interplay between emissions and environmental change governs the overall bioaccumulation and exposure. The situation becomes complicated considering there are different food-webs around the world. This ambitious project will seek to develop a global model describing bioaccumulation of trace contaminants in marine food-webs. This model will also be combined with other global modelling works being carried out in the group. Computational proficiency. Ability to conceptualize problems into workable sub-problems. Available to UGC-JRF and external only
